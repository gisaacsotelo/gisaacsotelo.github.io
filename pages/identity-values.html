Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

Situation: I was a kid around 11ish, I was in a water park with my family and some cousins, suddenly my sister finds some money under the water, bit or merky water so we where unable to see the bottom of it, I decided to get my googles and went for the hunt!, this ended up in me finding a lot of money around 5k Mexcian pesos (thats around 3.2 life fourtnes when you are 11).
I told my dad about this massive pack of money i gather bill by bill alongside with some hotel card and and a some keys (No IDs or anything to help know who the owner was)
My dad after seeing the amount told me to not give it back to the park authorities (Well this is Mexico, he told me they would just take it a dont give it to the owner), then he send me on the mission of finding someone that looks worried sick, and ask him about what was he was looking for, after a few hours I find the guy it was obviously him, just as dad said he lookes worried sick, and checking everywhere I went to ask him about what was he looking for, then he told me about the lost money and I said I only find 200 pesos instead of 5k, he just ignored me after.
My cousins where telling me to dont go and tell my dad about him that way I was going top be able to keep all that money, and buy a skateboard (I really wanted a sakteboard I remember that clearly).
At the end I went to my dad told him I found, the guy, we spoke with him asked a few questions (like which hotel was he staying and the amount he loose, dad just make sure he was the owner), then we gave it back, the guy was so happy jumping he even hugged me and thanks me so much for that.
My cosuing made fun of me for ages after that saying that I was just dumb.

I never regreted that decision, it really marked me and believe is one of the key experiences that made me who I am and what kinda of man my father was too, till this day my cousins remember that story and still think that was really dumb.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

A bit mixed with the past story, we have lots of saying back home, and well lots of corruption as I imagine everyone knows.
One of the most common sayings says: "El que no tranza, no avanza" (meaning the ones who doesnt cheat doesnt move forward), believe or not I do feel most of the people in the country lives by this rule, and took me years honestly years, to understand the values that my parents where trying to get on me.
No one looks after the good guy, at least not in my country for what I can remmeber, its quite sad sometimes.

It was until I was working here in New zealand on my first year where I realized the weight on those values I was taught, here people was just different, dont want to say better since it is not in me to decide that, there is great people everywhere, but it was till here when it finally clicked.
A part of me honestly believes that my parents groomed me to go away of the country.

I feel my culture showed me both sides of the coin, there are many, many thing I miss from back home, things that I honestly see as better from back home, but also lots and lots thta just feel better here, I believe is about of getting the good from where you find it and keep that and teach that, use the bad experiences to grow and learn.
